# Content Structure Model: Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics Course Overview

## Content Entities

### 1. Course Overview Module
- **Name**: Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics Course Overview
- **Description**: Foundational module establishing understanding before deeper modules (ROS2 → Digital Twins → Isaac Simulation → VLA policies)
- **Target Audience**: Advanced undergraduates, graduate students, and industry professionals
- **Validation Rules**: Must be 1000-1500 words, include 3-5 bolded terms, be academically rigorous yet accessible

### 2. Physical AI Introduction
- **Name**: Physical AI Fundamentals
- **Description**: Foundational concepts of Physical AI, distinguishing from traditional software-only AI systems
- **Content Elements**:
  - Definition of Physical AI
  - Distinction from traditional AI
  - Role of physical embodiment and environmental interaction
  - Examples of physical interaction influencing intelligent behavior
- **Validation Rules**: Must include at least 3 concrete examples of how physical interaction influences intelligent behavior

### 3. Embodied Intelligence
- **Name**: Embodied Intelligence Concepts
- **Description**: Understanding how intelligence emerges from the interaction between an agent and its environment
- **Content Elements**:
  - Definition of embodied intelligence
  - Dynamic coupling concepts
  - Body-environment interaction principles
  - Connection to Digital Twins and Sim-to-Real challenges
- **Validation Rules**: Must connect concepts to future modules on Digital Twins and Sim-to-Real challenges

### 4. Humanoid Robotics Landscape
- **Name**: Major Players in Humanoid Robotics
- **Description**: Overview of current state of humanoid robotics development with industry landscape analysis
- **Content Elements**:
  - Company (name of organization)
  - Platform (robot platform/model)
  - Focus Area (primary application domain)
  - Year Founded (company establishment date)
  - Current Status (current development/availability status)
  - Connection to Digital Twins/Sim-to-Real Challenges (relevance to future modules)
- **Validation Rules**: Must include at least 7 major companies with all specified columns

### 5. Sensor Systems
- **Name**: Sensor Systems in Humanoid Robotics
- **Description**: Understanding different types of sensors used in humanoid robotics
- **Content Elements**:
  - Proprioceptive sensors (internal state monitoring)
  - Exteroceptive sensors (environment perception)
  - Sensor fusion concepts
  - Sim-to-real transfer challenges
- **Validation Rules**: Must distinguish between proprioceptive and exteroceptive sensors and explain their respective roles

### 6. Societal Impact
- **Name**: Applications and Societal Impact
- **Description**: Real-world applications of Physical AI and humanoid robotics
- **Content Elements**:
  - Elder care applications
  - Logistics applications
  - Industrial automation applications
  - Human-robot interaction implications
- **Validation Rules**: Must include specific examples for each application area

## Content Relationships

### Sequential Dependencies
- Physical AI Introduction → Embodied Intelligence (foundational concepts first)
- Embodied Intelligence → Humanoid Robotics Landscape (application of concepts)
- Humanoid Robotics Landscape → Sensor Systems (technical implementation details)
- All modules → Societal Impact (broader implications)

### Cross-Module Connections
- All modules must include explicit connections to Digital Twins (Module 2) and Sim-to-Real challenges (Module 3)
- Index module provides roadmap to all other modules and call-to-action toward Module 1 (ROS2)

## Content States

### Draft State
- Initial content creation with basic structure
- Placeholder content where research is still needed

### Review State
- Content reviewed for technical accuracy
- Cross-verification with authoritative sources completed

### Approved State
- Content validated for Docusaurus compatibility
- All requirements from specification met
- Ready for publication in textbook
